VAPOR LABS CLOSES $12 MILLION SERIES A TO BUILD THE OPERATING SYSTEM 
FOR THE NETWORKED ECONOMY

Sequoia Capital Leads Round; Company Valued at $50 Million Pre-Revenue

SAN FRANCISCO, CA â€” Vapor Labs, the pioneering platform company 
revolutionizing B2B2C value chain integration, today announced the close 
of a $12 million Series A funding round led by Sequoia Capital, with 
participation from Kleiner Perkins and angel investors including several 
former Netscape executives.

"We've seen a lot of pitches, but Marcus and the Vapor Labs team have 
articulated a vision that genuinely excites us," said Michael Hartley, 
Partner at Sequoia Capital. "VaporWare has the potential to become the 
essential infrastructure layer for internet commerce. We're proud to 
support this journey."

The funding significantly exceeded the company's initial $5 million target, 
reflecting strong investor confidence in the VaporWare platform vision.

"This validation from tier-one investors confirms what we've known from 
the beginning: the market is ready for VaporWare," said Marcus Chen, CEO 
of Vapor Labs. "With this funding, we'll accelerate our roadmap and bring 
VaporWare 1.0 to market by Q4 1999."

The company plans to use the funds to:
- Expand the engineering team to 50+ employees
- Open a new 15,000 square foot headquarters in SOMA
- Launch the VaporWare 1.0 beta program with select enterprise partners
- Build out sales and marketing capabilities

ABOUT VAPOR LABS

Vapor Labs is architecting the foundational infrastructure layer for 
next-generation digital experiences. The company's flagship VaporWare 
platform enables seamless value creation across the entire spectrum of 
human endeavor through proprietary synergy-optimization technology.
