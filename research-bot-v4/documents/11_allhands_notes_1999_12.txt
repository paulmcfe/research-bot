VAPOR LABS ALL-HANDS MEETING NOTES
December 3, 1999
Location: New Auditorium, 3rd Floor
Attendees: All Hands (~180 employees)
Notes by: Sarah Chen, Executive Assistant

---

OPENING REMARKS - Marcus Chen, CEO

Marcus began with our biggest news: the Series B close. "$85 million 
at a $400 million valuation. We are now one of the most valuable 
private internet companies in the Bay Area."

He thanked the team for the incredible year. "In January, we were 4 
people with a PowerPoint. In December, we're 180 people with customers, 
partners, and enough funding to change the world. That's the power of 
vision plus execution."

Marcus acknowledged some challenges. "I know the last few months have 
been intense. The dual-track product strategy has stretched us. Some 
of you have been working weekends. That's not sustainable forever, 
and we're hiring as fast as we can to provide relief."

He announced the company holiday party on December 17th at the W Hotel. 
"Open bar. Great food. Plus-ones welcome. You've earned it."

---

PRODUCT UPDATE - Diana Reeves, CTO

Diana provided a candid update on product status.

VaporWare Enterprise:
- Alpha is complete
- Currently in beta with 8 Fortune 500 companies
- Feedback is "generally positive with significant issues"
- Most common complaints: performance, documentation, complexity
- 1.0 timeline: Slipping from Q1 to Q2 2000

"I want to be honest with you," Diana said. "We tried to do too much, 
too fast. The architecture changes to support the consumer platform 
have cost us months. We're stabilizing now, but it's been painful."

VaporWare Personal:
- Tyler Morrison (VP Consumer) presented this section
- Team has grown to 45 dedicated engineers
- Vapor Studio alpha is "close to feature-complete"
- Vapor Marketplace backend is 60% complete
- Vapor Community is "in early development"
- Beta launch targeting Q2 2000

Tyler showed a demo of Vapor Studio. The audience was impressed by 
the visual editor, though several people noted the Java Applet took 
over 30 seconds to load.

Q: Are we going to hit Q2 for both products?
Diana: It's going to be close. We're prioritizing Enterprise because 
that's where the revenue contracts are.
Tyler: We can hit Q2 for a limited beta if we cut scope.
Marcus: We'll figure it out. Next question.

---

SALES UPDATE - Jordan Blake, VP BD

Jordan reported on enterprise pipeline:
- 8 active beta customers
- 15 more in "late-stage conversations"
- 3 signed letters of intent totaling $4M in year-one revenue
- Average deal size tracking toward $1.5M

"The demand is real," Jordan said. "Our challenge isn't finding 
customers—it's having a product ready for them to buy."

Jordan also announced we've signed partnership agreements with Oracle, 
Sun, and Cisco. "These aren't just press releases. These are real 
go-to-market commitments."

New hire: Jordan introduced Steve Morrison (no relation to Tyler), 
new VP of Enterprise Sales, joining from Siebel.

---

MARKETING UPDATE - Rachel Torres, VP Marketing

Rachel showed coverage highlights:
- Feature in Red Herring "Top 100 Companies to Watch"
- Mention in Wired "The New Economy's New Players"
- Business 2.0 interview with Marcus (cover story pending)
- Internet World booth generated 1,200+ leads

"We've established VaporWare as a serious player," Rachel said. "Now 
we need to deliver on the promise."

She previewed the 2000 marketing plan:
- Super Bowl ad (30 seconds, still negotiating pricing)
- Massive launch event for VaporWare 1.0
- Analyst relations program with Gartner and Forrester
- Customer conference in Q4 2000 ("VaporCon")

Estimated marketing budget for 2000: $30 million.

Q: A Super Bowl ad? Seriously?
Rachel: Half the companies in our category have Super Bowl ads. We 
need to play at that level.
Marcus: It's not about the ad. It's about signaling that we're real. 
When your parents see VaporWare on the Super Bowl, they'll know we've 
made it.

---

OPERATIONS UPDATE - New Role!

Marcus introduced Maria Santos, newly hired VP of Operations.

Maria: "My job is to make sure the trains run on time. As we grow, 
the things that used to just happen—office space, equipment, IT 
systems—those need real processes. I'm building that infrastructure."

Current priorities:
- Opening New York office (January)
- Opening London office (March)
- Upgrading internal systems (email, file sharing, etc.)
- Establishing Exodus data center relationship for hosting

---

HR UPDATE - Jennifer Williams, Head of HR

Jennifer reviewed headcount:
- Current: 180 employees
- Target end of 2000: 500+ employees
- "We're hiring aggressively in engineering, sales, and operations"

New policies for 2000:
- Parental leave increased to 12 weeks paid
- Work-from-home policy in development
- Employee stock purchase plan launching Q1

Stock options update: 409A valuation increased to $3.00/share (up 
from $0.50 at Series A). "Your options are worth 6x what they were 
six months ago."

---

FINANCE UPDATE - David Park, CFO (New!)

Marcus introduced David Park, newly hired CFO from KPMG.

David: "I'm here to make sure we're good stewards of the $97 million 
we have in the bank. We're investing aggressively in growth, but we're 
also watching the burn rate carefully."

Current burn rate: Approximately $4 million per month
Runway: 24+ months at current burn

David announced a new expense policy: "All expenses over $10,000 need 
VP approval. Over $50,000 needs CFO approval. We're not becoming 
bureaucratic, but we need basic controls as we scale."

---

Q&A

Q: There are rumors about disagreements between Diana and Tyler on 
product direction. Can you address that?
Marcus: There's no conflict. We have passionate leaders with different 
perspectives. That's healthy. We've aligned on a unified platform 
vision, and we're executing.
(Diana and Tyler nodded but did not make eye contact)

Q: Are we really doing a Super Bowl ad when the product isn't done?
Marcus: The ad airs February 6th. Enterprise 1.0 ships by then. 
That's the plan, and we're going to hit it.

Q: I've been working 70-hour weeks for three months. When does it 
get better?
Marcus: I hear you, and I appreciate the sacrifice. We're hiring as 
fast as we can. In the meantime, use your unlimited PTO. Take care 
of yourselves. We're running a marathon, not a sprint. (Pause) Well, 
maybe a really long sprint.

Q: What's the IPO timeline?
David (CFO): We can't comment on that. But I can say we're building 
the financial infrastructure that would be required for a public 
company. Draw your own conclusions.

---

CLOSING

Marcus: "1999 was our year of foundation. 2000 is our year of growth. 
We're going to launch products, acquire customers, and establish 
VaporWare as the platform that powers the networked economy. It's 
going to be intense. It's going to be hard. And it's going to be the 
most exciting ride of your careers. Let's do this."

Cheers and applause. Meeting ended at 12:30 PM.

Holiday Party: December 17th, W Hotel, 7 PM

---
Sarah Chen
Executive Assistant to the CEO
